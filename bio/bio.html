About Me

Hi! My name is Patrick and I'm a front-end engineer based in Houston, TX. I recently graduated from nursing school in May of 2021. Instead of staying in medicine and pursuing nursing, I decided to spend the next few months learning how to create websites and web applications in order to become a front-end engineer. Over the past year I have studied Javascript, HTML, CSS, React and Node through various resources including Udemy and Generation USA. I have received certificates for HTML/CSS/Javascript courses, and now I am focusing on learning React in my free time. I'm also a Software Engineer Apprentice employed by Intermountain Healthcare, and I was able to attain this apprenticeship through Multiverse.

I love to play, study and watch basketball.

Ever since I can remember basketball has been a part of my life in some way. Whether it was playing in my neighborhood with all my friends as a child. We would stay outside and play until the street lights came on and our parents called us inside. Coming outside the next day to finish those games we started to see who would come out on top.

My love for basketball led to me playing in middle school and high school where I got to develop my skills even further and challenge myself to play at a higher level. I even got to witness my school when the state championship that year, I was pretty luck to watch games at that level and cheer on my school.

Now as an adult I watch mostly NBA games and cheer for the Houston Rockets sometimes in person but mostly on TV. I also watch a ton of content on YouTube and I like to watch film breaking down NBA games.

Reading is one of my favorite things to do!

Reading is one of my early and favorite memories from school. My love for reading originally came from books like Magic Tree House, Diary of a Wimpy Kid, the Percy Jackson series and so much more. I recognized early that reading was like a movie playing in my mind that I could take anywhere with me.

I always loved the imagery from these creative books and the hobby found me again in adulthood. Now some of my favorite books to date have been Deep Work by Cal Newport, Mastery by Robert Greene and Animal Farm by George Orwell.
